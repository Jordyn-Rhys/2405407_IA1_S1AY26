Projects

Virtual Machine

This project is a simple virtual machine implemented in C. It uses an instruction set that includes operations such as push (PSH), add (ADD), pop (POP), set (SET), and halt (HLT). The program simulates a stack-based execution where instructions manipulate a stack array, handling values with a stack pointer. The main loop fetches and evaluates instructions until the halt command stops execution. This virtual machine demonstrates basic principles of instruction execution and stack operations in a minimalistic environment.

Hash Table

This project is a hash table implementation in C. It includes functionality to create new hash table items, insert, search, and delete key-value pairs efficiently. The hash table dynamically resizes based on its load to maintain performance, using prime number sizes for better distribution. It uses double hashing with two prime constants for collision resolution. The implementation leverages helper functions from a prime number module to handle sizing and resizing. Overall, this project provides a robust data structure for fast key-based lookup and management.

Building a Life of Intention

Chapter 4: Achieve your Goals

This chapter emphasizes the importance of setting clear, specific goals and pursuing them with discipline. Jim Rohn and Chris Widener argue that goals give life direction and purpose. Without them, we drift. The chapter encourages readers to write down their goals, break them into actionable steps, and review them regularly. For me, this resonates deeply; I often juggle multiple creative and technical projects, and having written goals would help me prioritize and stay focused. Aligning this with my life means treating each design challenge or coding milestone as a goal with a deadline, not just a task.

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time

Time is described as one of our most valuable resources. The authors stress that successful people don’t necessarily have more time—they just use it better. This chapter challenges readers to audit how they spend their hours and eliminate distractions. As someone who values detail and iteration in my work, I sometimes get caught in perfectionism. Applying this pillar means setting boundaries around my creative process—knowing when to refine and when to ship. It also means carving out time for learning and rest, not just production.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself with the Best People

Here, the authors explore the power of influence. The people we associate with shape our mindset, habits, and ultimately our outcomes. The chapter encourages readers to seek mentors, build uplifting relationships, and distance themselves from negativity. This hits home for me—collaboration is central to my growth, and I’ve seen firsthand how working with thoughtful, driven peers elevates my own standards. Aligning this to my life means being intentional about who I learn from, who I share ideas with, and who I allow to challenge me constructively.

Chapter 7: Be a Life Long Learner

This chapter champions curiosity and continuous education. Rohn and Widener argue that personal development is the foundation of success. Whether through books, podcasts, or conversations, learning should be a daily habit. For me, this is already a core value—I thrive when I’m experimenting with new layouts, testing responsive techniques, or exploring visual effects. But this chapter reminds me to go beyond just technical skills and invest in emotional intelligence, leadership, and communication. Aligning this pillar means treating every project not just as output, but as a learning opportunity.
